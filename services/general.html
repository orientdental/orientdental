At the heart of every healthy smile is prevention, precision, and patient care — general dentistry is where confident smiles begin.

General dentistry focuses on the prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of common dental problems. It forms the foundation of overall oral health, addressing everything from cavities and gum disease to emergency care.

Common Treatments

  • Fillings (Composite or Amalgam) – For treating cavities
  • Tooth Extractions – For severely decayed or damaged teeth
  • Root Canal Treatment – To save infected teeth
  • Dental Cleaning (Scaling & Polishing) – To prevent gum disease
  • Emergency Dental Care – For sudden pain, broken teeth, or infections

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a dental filling painful?

No. Fillings are done under local anesthesia and are usually pain-free. Some sensitivity may occur afterward.

How do I know if I need a root canal?

Common signs include pain when chewing, prolonged sensitivity to hot or cold, and swelling near the tooth.

Is tooth extraction safe?

Yes, when performed by a professional. Most extractions are simple and heal quickly with proper care.

How often should I get a dental cleaning?

Most dentists recommend cleanings every 6 months to prevent plaque buildup and gum disease.

What should I do during a dental emergency?

Call the clinic immediately. If it involves swelling, pain, or a broken tooth, prompt treatment is crucial.
