Dentistry is more than restoring teeth — it's about giving patients their smile back, their voice, and the freedom to live and eat without hesitation.

Dental prosthetics are devices used to restore missing or damaged teeth. They improve both function and aesthetics. Patients choose prosthetics to regain chewing ability, restore confidence, and improve speech.

Types of Dental Prosthetics

  • Crowns (Zirconia, Porcelain, E-max)
  • Bridges (3-unit, full-arch)
  • Full Dentures
  • Partial Dentures
  • Flexible Dentures
  • Implant-supported Dentures

Common Materials

  • Zirconia – highly aesthetic and strong
  • Porcelain fused to metal (PFM) – reliable and affordable
  • Acrylic – used for temporary solutions
  • Titanium – used in implant-supported bars

How the Prosthetic Procedure Works

Consultation & Impressions: The dentist examines the case and takes dental impressions.
1
Tooth Preparation: If needed, teeth are reshaped to accommodate crowns or bridges.
2
Lab Fabrication: The prosthetic is crafted in the lab. This takes 3–7 days depending on the material.
3
Fitting & Adjustments: The dentist fits the prosthesis and checks bite, comfort, and aesthetics.
4
Aftercare Instructions: Patients receive guidance on maintenance and hygiene.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do crowns or bridges last?

With proper care, dental crowns and bridges can last 10 to 15 years or longer. Materials like zirconia are especially durable and resistant to wear.

Is zirconia better than porcelain?

Yes. Zirconia is stronger, more aesthetic, and more biocompatible, often preferred for long-term restorations.

Will it feel natural?

Yes. Our prosthetics are custom-designed to fit comfortably and restore natural chewing function.

Can I eat normally with crowns or dentures?

Absolutely. Crowns and bridges allow eating all types of food after adaptation. Removable dentures may take a few days to get used to.

What warranty do you offer?

We provide a 2–5 year warranty depending on the prosthetic type and materials used.
