Oral Surgery – Restoring Health and Comfort

Oral surgery covers a wide range of procedures, from simple tooth extractions to complex surgical treatments, ensuring your oral health and long-term comfort.

Common Oral Surgeries

  • Tooth Extractions – including wisdom teeth
  • Dental Implants – surgical placement of titanium roots
  • Gum Surgery – to treat periodontal disease
  • Bone Grafting – rebuilding jawbone for implants
  • Oral Biopsies – diagnostic tissue sampling

How Oral Surgery Works

1. Examination & Imaging: X-rays and scans help plan the surgery safely.
2. Anesthesia: Local or general anesthesia is given to ensure comfort.
3. Surgical Procedure: The dentist or surgeon performs the treatment with precision.
4. Recovery Monitoring: After the procedure, patients are observed until stable.
5. Aftercare & Healing: Detailed instructions for healing and follow-ups are provided.

  • Modern anesthesia and pain management
  • Safe, sterile surgical environment
  • Comprehensive aftercare support

Frequently Asked Questions

Is oral surgery painful?

With modern anesthesia, most procedures are comfortable and virtually painless.

How long is recovery time?

Most patients recover in a few days, but complex surgeries may require longer healing.

Can I go home the same day?

Yes, most oral surgeries are outpatient procedures with same-day discharge.

What should I eat after surgery?

Soft foods like yogurt, soups, and smoothies are recommended for the first few days.
